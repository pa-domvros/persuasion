What is Persuasive Writing? Overview

Persuasive writing is when you use your language skill to convince someone to agree with your opinion or take a specific action.

Tip: We can appeal to people in different ways. The three persuasive appeals are Logos, Pathos, and Ethos.

Logos Appeals to Logic

How does it work?

It encourages people to think rationally and use their logic. It strengthens an argument by using factual information.

Writing strategies
  • Use quotes from experts on the topic.
  • Mention well-known theories or facts.
  • Include reliable sources for data and statistics.
  • Use real-life examples to make the topic relatable.

Pathos Appeals to Emotion

How does it work?

It makes the audience feel specific emotions‚Äîoften fear, sadness, sympathy, happiness, or anger. It also supports ideals the audience strongly values.

Writing strategies
  • Use emotionally charged language and tone.
  • Mention anecdotes and stories.
  • Use vivid descriptions to paint a picture for your audience.
  • Use figurative language (simile, metaphor, hyperbole, etc.).

Ethos Appeals to Credibility

How does it work?

It builds trust and respect between the writer and the audience, strengthening the argument by establishing credibility.

Writing strategies
  • Use appropriate language, spelling, punctuation, and grammar to build personal credibility.
  • Reference credible sources of information.
  • Mention relevant awards and achievements.
